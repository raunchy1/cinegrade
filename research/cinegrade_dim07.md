## 1. Luxury Wedding Photography Trends: Soft, Dreamy, Film-like vs Editorial Contrast

The luxury wedding photography market in 2025 is split between two dominant aesthetic camps: **soft/dreamy film emulation** (epitomized by photographers like Samm Blake, Greg Finck, and those using Mastin Labs presets) and **high-contrast editorial/fashion-forward styling** (influenced by photographers like Jose Villa, Norman & Blake, and publications like Vogue Weddings).

**Context:** The split reflects a generational divide: older millennials often prefer the soft film look, while Gen Z couples ("poster bride Millie Bobby Brown") increasingly gravitate toward "vibe-driven" editorial photography with dramatic lighting and unconventional poses.

### Finding 3.2: The "Sam Kolder" Orange & Teal Look

The **orange and teal** color grading style (popularized by **Sam Kolder**) remains one of the most sought-after aesthetics in travel photography. It leverages complementary color theory: orange and teal sit opposite on the RGB color wheel. The technique boosts blues in shadows while enhancing oranges in midtones and highlights — making it especially effective for portraits and people-focused travel content.

**Source:** Kevin Raposo [^183^], Cinema LUTs [^271^], Fstoppers [^277^]  
**URL:** https://kevinraposo.com/a-guide-to-the-orange-and-teal-look/  
**Date:** 2024-03-13  
**Confidence:** HIGH

**Excerpt:**
> "Most human skin tones fall into the midtones and highlights category, which is why this color grading technique is so effective for portraits and people-focused photography. The trick is to boost blues in the shadows while enhancing oranges in the midtones and highlights." — Kevin Raposo [^183^]

**Technical Implementation:**
- Color Calibration: Shift blue primary hue toward teal
- Shadows color wheel: Teal/cyan, moderate saturation
- Highlights color wheel: Orange/amber, higher saturation
- Keep saturation below 15% to avoid unnatural results
- Preserve skin tone realism through selective correction

## 7. Food Photography in Luxury Context

### Finding 7.1: Food Color Grading by Category

Food photography color grading varies significantly by food type. The **critical rule** across all categories: keep saturation below 15% — "anything higher and the color grading becomes visible as an obvious filter rather than a subtle mood enhancer."

**Source:** FoodShot AI [^232^]  
**URL:** https://foodshot.ai/blog/food-color-grading  
**Date:** 2026-03-23  
**Confidence:** HIGH

**Excerpt:**
> "The critical rule: Keep saturation below 15% in food photography. Anything higher and the color grading becomes visible as an obvious filter rather than a subtle mood enhancer. Subtlety is everything." — FoodShot AI [^232^]

**Color Guidelines by Food Type:**

| Food Type | Hue | Saturation | Luminance | Notes |
|-----------|-----|------------|-----------|-------|
| Citrus/yellow | Shift toward orange | Yellow +10 to +15 | Yellow -15 to -20 | Adds richness without neon |
| Red sauces/meat | Shift red toward orange +5 to +10 | Red +5 to +10 | — | Add magenta in highlights |
| Greens (salad) | Keep centered or shift aqua | Reduce yellow -10 to -15 | Green +5 to +10 | Eliminates fluorescent look |
| Chocolate/coffee | Shift orange toward yellow | Orange +5 | Orange +10 to +15 | Golden quality in browns |

**Warm Comfort Food Grading:**
- Shadows: Warm orange/amber, saturation 8-12%
- Highlights: Golden yellow, saturation 5-8%
- Adds "golden glow that says 'homemade' and 'delicious'"

**Fresh/Clean Food Grading:**
- Shadows: Cool blue/teal, saturation 5-8%
- Highlights: Neutral or barely-there warm, saturation 3-5%
- Creates "crisp, clean feeling without making food look cold"

### Finding 7.2: White Background Food Photography

White background food photography requires special techniques: **expose for background then recover food**, use AI masking (Select Subject) to grade food and background independently, monitor RGB values for neutral white (within 2-3 points), and push whites until background reads RGB 245-250.

**Source:** FoodShot AI [^232^]  
**Confidence:** HIGH

## 8. Skin Tone Handling Across Ethnicities

### Finding 8.1: The Technical Challenge

Photographing couples with different skin tones presents a **dynamic range challenge**: if exposing for darker skin, lighter skin gets blown out; if exposing for lighter skin, darker skin becomes muddy. Modern mirrorless cameras (Sony Alpha, Canon R-series, Nikon Z-series) shooting in RAW are essential.

**Source:** Salt & Stone Adventure Photo [^311^]  
**URL:** https://saltandstoneadventurephoto.com/2025/07/23/how-to-choose-a-photographer-who-can-accurately-capture-mixed-race-and-biracial-couples-skin-tones/  
**Date:** 2025-07-23  
**Confidence:** HIGH

**Excerpt:**
> "In biracial couples, one partner may have very fair skin, the other much deeper. If your photographer exposes for the darker skin tone, the fairer skin often gets blown out. If they expose for the lighter tone, the deeper skin tone can become muddy or flat." — Salt & Stone [^311^]

**Technical Solution:**
1. **In-camera:** Slightly underexpose to protect highlights, recover shadow detail in post
2. **Post-production:** Use selective editing (masking) — lift shadows on deeper skin, adjust warmth individually, preserve luminous glow on lighter skin
3. **Critical:** Never try to "match" skin tones — honor them both individually

---

### Finding 8.2: Photographing Dark Skin at Weddings

Professional photographer **Benson** shares that photographing melanated skin requires understanding that "light and lighting play a huge part." She dislikes golden hour for dark skin because "all you get is yellow and you don't see any other color." Instead, she brings **additional light sources** (external flash, reflector, softbox, diffuser) to control white light and white balance that brings back color.

**Source:** The Knot [^291^]  
**URL:** https://www.theknot.com/content/photographing-dark-skin-tones  
**Date:** 2022-01-21  
**Confidence:** HIGH

**Excerpt:**
> "I always bring an additional light source like an external flash and maybe a reflector, softbox or diffuser just to have control of the white light and white balance that brings back color. I add as much light as I possibly can for my melanated clients and then get creative in post-production." — Benson [^291^]

**Key Insights:**
- Expose for highlights (highlights maintain detail and color)
- Add as much controlled light as possible
- Spend extra time on individual brushstrokes/radial filters
- One-click presets are insufficient for mixed skin tone weddings

---

### Finding 8.3: Identifying Skin Overtones and Undertones

All skin presents in "innumerable tones, shades, hues and saturations." The two main aspects are **overtones** (fair, tan, medium, deep, deep dark) and **undertones** (warm: peachy neutral to golden/orange-red; cool: neutral to pink-red). Accurate portrayal requires identifying undertones to avoid making subjects appear ashy or orange.

## 9. Golden Hour & Blue Hour Grading Preferences

### Finding 9.1: Golden Hour — The Wedding Photography Gold Standard

Golden hour light measures approximately **3,000 Kelvin** and is prized for three reasons: the light is softer (sun at angle, diffused), directional (low angle, longer shadows, creative possibilities for rim lighting/backlighting), and warm (yellows, oranges, reds — atmosphere filters out blue light). During golden hour, "skin tones get washed out by really direct sunlight. But the golden hue makes for beautiful skin tones that are hard to blow out."

**Source:** Adobe [^282^], Nature TTL [^278^]  
**URL:** https://www.adobe.com/creativecloud/photography/technique/golden-hour.html  
**Date:** 2026-01-12  
**Confidence:** HIGH

**Excerpt:**
> "It's hard to overexpose or underexpose parts of an image, like a subject's face, because the light is very even and has a beautiful golden hue... Skin tones get washed out by really direct sunlight. But the golden hue makes for beautiful skin tones that are hard to blow out." — Jenn Byrne, Adobe [^282^]

**Editing Golden Hour Photos:**
- White balance: 3,000K baseline; adjust to intensify or neutralize warmth
- Set white and black points for contrast and depth
- Recover highlights in white/partially white subjects
- Lift shadows to reveal detail
- Gentle S-curve for dimension

### Finding 9.3: The Teal & Orange Cinematic Look

The teal and orange (or "orange and teal") look uses **complementary color theory**: shadows pushed toward teal/cyan, highlights toward orange/amber. This works because most human skin tones fall in the midtone/highlight range, while shadows and environmental elements skew cool. Four methods in Lightroom: Color Calibration (shift blue primary toward teal), Tone Curve (reverse S-curve on blues), HSL adjustments, and Split Toning/Color Grading.

**Source:** Kevin Raposo [^183^], Cinema LUTs [^271^], Fstoppers [^277^]  
**URL:** https://kevinraposo.com/a-guide-to-the-orange-and-teal-look/  
**Date:** 2024-03-13  
**Confidence:** HIGH

**Color Grading Settings (Lightroom):**
- Shadows: Teal hue, moderate saturation
- Highlights: Orange/amber, higher saturation
- Midtones: Slight orange/red for smooth blend
- Balance slider: Slightly positive (+10 to +20)
- Keep saturation restrained to avoid unnatural results

## Summary: Key Color Grading Principles for CINEGRADE LAB

### Universal Principles Across All 12 Sub-Dimensions

1. **Subtlety is everything** — Saturation below 15% for food; restraint in all luxury contexts
2. **Skin tone preservation** — The most critical factor; selective editing via masking
3. **Film emulation is the luxury signifier** — Mastin Labs, Fuji 400H, Portra 400 are gold standards
4. **Environment-specific grading** — Santorini blues, Tuscany golds, Iceland moody desaturation
5. **The orange-teal axis** — Foundational to cinematic travel and editorial wedding looks
6. **Golden hour warmth** — ~3,000K baseline; the single most valued lighting condition
7. **Blue hour romance** — Pastel, cinematic, increasingly searched on Pinterest
8. **Clean/minimal = luxury** — Whether clean girl lifestyle or editorial negative space
9. **Hybrid approaches win** — Film + digital, editorial + documentary, warm + cool balance
10. **Celebrity influence is immediate** — Samm Blake, Jose Villa styles replicated within months
